**Date of Publication:** [ 8th March, 1926 ]

## Preamble

WHEREAS doubts have arisen as to the powers of [1][the High Court Division] to punish contempts of Courts;AND WHEREAS it is expedient to resolve these doubts and to define and limit the powers exercisable by [the High Court Division] in punishing contempt of Court; It is hereby enacted as follows:-

## Sections/Articles

### Short title, extent and commencement

1. (1)This Act may be called the [Contempt of Courts Act, 1926](/act-140.html "Contempt of Courts Act, 1926").(2) It extends to the whole of [2](1)[Bangladesh].(3) It shall come into force on such date as the [3](2)[Government] may, by notification in the official Gazette, appoint.

### Power of superior Courts to punish contempts of court

2. (1) Subject to the provisions of sub-section (3), the [4](1)[High Court Division] shall have and exercise the same Jurisdiction, powers and authority, in accordance with the same procedure and practice, in respect of contempt of courts subordinate to [5](2)[it as it has and exercises in respect of contempts of itself].(2) [6](3)[Omitted by section 4 of the Contempt of Courts (Amendment) Ordinance, 1959 (Ordinance No. XLV of 1959).](3) [7](4)[The High Court Division shall not] take cognizance of a contempt alleged to have been committed in respect of a court subordinate to it where such contempt is an offence punishable under the [Penal Code].

### Limit of punishment for contempt of court

3. Save as otherwise expressly provided by any law for the time being in force, a contempt of court may be punished with simple imprisonment for a term which may extend to six months, or with fine, which may extend to two thousand [8](1)[Taka], or with bothProvided that the accused may be discharged or the punishment awarded may be remitted on apology being made to the satisfaction of the Court:Provided further that notwithstanding anything else where contained in any law [9](2)[the High Court Division shall not] impose a sentence in excess of that specified in this section for any contempt either in respect of itself or of a Court subordinate to it.
